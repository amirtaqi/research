About

I am a Materials Informatics Scientist at Solid Power Operating Inc., where I work at the intersection of data-driven modeling, multiscale simulation, and battery materials design. My current focus is on applying computational materials science and informatics to accelerate the development and deployment of next-generation energy storage technologies.

I received my PhD in Mechanical & Industrial Engineering from Northeastern University, where my dissertation developed multiscale computational frameworks to design carbon- and iron-based materials for electrochemical water treatment and carbon capture applications. Earlier in my career, I worked on nanofluidics, interfacial transport, and 2D materials at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign, including graphene-based biosensors and transport in nanoscale channels.

Broadly, my research spans electrochemical systems, materials informatics, water and environmental remediation, CO2 sequestration, nanofluidics, and 2D materials. I am currently on the academic job market and seeking a faculty position in mechanical engineering, materials science, chemical/environmental engineering, or related fields.

Research

My research combines computational materials science, atomistic and continuum simulations, and data-driven modeling to engineer electrochemical systems for water treatment, CO2 sequestration, and energy storage. A recurring theme in my work is connecting molecular-scale phenomena to device- and system-level performance.

Electrochemical Water Treatment & ROS Generation

I develop models and frameworks to design granular activated carbon (GAC) and other electrodes that efficiently generate reactive oxidative species (ROS) for water remediation. This includes multiscale modeling of structure–porosity relationships, transport, and electrochemical kinetics in flow-through reactors.

  • Multiscale modeling of GAC electrodes and water adsorption
  • Design of materials for in situ H2O2 and ·OH generation

CO2 Sequestration & Ocean Electrochemistry

I work on electrochemical strategies that couple iron chemistry, alkalinity enhancement, and carbon capture, including ocean iron fertilization concepts. My work explores how engineered materials and electrochemical processes can support large-scale, durable CO2 sequestration.

  • Electrochemical ocean iron fertilization and alkalinity enhancement
  • Iron-based electrochemical systems for CO2 removal

Nanofluidics, 2D Materials & Biosensing

Earlier work focused on transport in nanoscale channels and 2D heterostructures, including strongly coupled electroosmotic flows and high-sensitivity biosensors based on deformed graphene and MoS2 heterostructures.

  • Electroosmotic coupling in nanoscale channels
  • Graphene-based field-effect biosensors for nucleic acids

Materials Informatics & Solid-State Batteries

At Solid Power, I apply AI and informatics tools to solid-state battery materials and cells, bridging laboratory testing and field applications for advanced energy storage.

  • Data-driven workflows for materials and cell performance prediction
  • Integration of experimental, simulation, and field data in battery development
