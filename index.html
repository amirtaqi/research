About

I am a Materials Informatics Scientist at Solid Power Operating Inc., where I work at the intersection of data-driven modeling, multiscale simulation, and battery materials design. My focus is on applying computational materials science and informatics to accelerate the development of next-generation energy storage technologies.

I received my PhD in Mechanical & Industrial Engineering from Northeastern University, where my dissertation developed multiscale computational frameworks for carbon- and iron-based materials used in electrochemical water treatment and carbon capture. I previously worked on nanofluidics, interfacial transport, and 2D materials at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign, including graphene biosensors and transport in nanoscale channels.

My research spans electrochemical systems, materials informatics, CO2 sequestration, nanofluidics, and 2D materials. I am currently seeking a faculty position in mechanical engineering, materials science, or related fields.

Research

My research combines computational materials science, atomistic and continuum simulations, and data-driven modeling to engineer electrochemical systems for water treatment, CO2 sequestration, and energy storage. A recurring theme is connecting molecular-scale phenomena to system-level performance.

Electrochemical Water Treatment & ROS Generation

I develop frameworks to design granular activated carbon (GAC) and other electrodes that generate reactive oxidative species for water remediation.

  • Multiscale modeling of GAC electrodes
  • Design for in situ H2O2 and ·OH generation

CO2 Sequestration & Ocean Electrochemistry

I explore electrochemical systems coupling iron chemistry, alkalinity, and carbon capture for large-scale CO2 sequestration.

  • Electrochemical iron fertilization and alkalinity enhancement
  • Iron-based CO2 removal systems

Nanofluidics, 2D Materials & Biosensing

Earlier work focused on transport in nanoscale channels and 2D heterostructures such as graphene-based biosensors.

  • Electroosmotic coupling in nanofluidic channels
  • Graphene biosensors for nucleic acid detection

Materials Informatics & Solid-State Batteries

At Solid Power, I apply AI and informatics tools to predict material and cell-level performance in solid-state batteries.

  • Data-driven workflows for performance prediction
  • Integration of simulations and field data
