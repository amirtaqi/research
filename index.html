Research focus Multiscale ML & Thermo-Fluid–Electrochemical Systems

I work at the intersection of multiscale thermo-fluid–electrochemical modeling and machine learning, with an emphasis on energy and environmental systems: batteries, electrochemical materials, and reactors for water and climate applications.

My goal is to develop physics-based and data-driven tools that connect materials, microstructures, and devices to system-level performance, safety, and reliability, enabling better understanding and more informed engineering decisions.

Multiscale modeling & simulation
Materials → cells → devices → systems
Physics-informed ML & data fusion

Main areas

  • Batteries & energy storage: electro-thermal–mechanical modeling, data-driven state and health estimation, and safety-aware design.
  • Electrochemical materials: porous carbons, solid electrolytes, and composite electrodes where microstructure controls coupled transport.
  • Water & climate technologies: electrochemical water treatment, CO2 removal, and ocean-electrochemical processes.

Across these areas, I integrate continuum models, high-fidelity simulations, and data-driven surrogates with experiments and diagnostics.

Research Energy, Materials, and Electrochemical Systems

My research uses thermo-fluid–electrochemical models and machine learning to understand and design energy and environmental systems. Examples include solid-state and advanced batteries, materials for water treatment and CO2 removal, and nanofluidic and 2D-material systems.

Batteries & Energy Storage

Electro-thermal–mechanical behavior, degradation, and data-driven diagnostics

  • Coupled electrochemical, thermal, and mechanical models for advanced chemistries and cell/pack architectures.
  • Physics-informed ML and reduced-order models for state-of-health and performance prediction.
  • Use of laboratory and field data to inform models and identify key degradation pathways and operating constraints.

Industry experience at Solid Power, Nissan, and IBM coupling modeling and ML with experimental data and diagnostics.

Electrochemical Materials & Microstructures

Microstructure, transport, and material design for electrochemical systems

  • Multiscale modeling of porous carbons, solid electrolytes, and composite electrodes, including transport of ions, electrons, and heat.
  • Image-based analysis and microstructure informatics for relating structure to effective properties and durability.
  • Use of simulation and curated datasets to support the design of materials for energy and environmental applications.

Prior work spans granular activated carbon electrodes, nanopore hydrodynamics, and 2D-material-based sensing platforms.

Electrochemical Reactors for Water & Climate

Flow, transport, and electrochemistry in water treatment and CO2 removal

  • Modeling of electrochemical water treatment systems that generate reactive oxidative species (ROS) using engineered electrodes.
  • Ocean-electrochemical concepts such as electrochemical ocean iron fertilization and alkalinity enhancement for CO2 sequestration.
  • Thermo-fluid–electrochemical analysis of energy use, removal efficiency, and operating conditions.

Work includes studies of ROS-generating materials, reactor configurations, and ocean-based electrochemical approaches.

Cross-Cutting Methods

Modeling, ML, and data practices that support all of the above

  • Physics-informed ML, neural operators, and reduced-order models that respect conservation laws and constraints.
  • Uncertainty quantification, Bayesian inference, and data assimilation for combining models with heterogeneous measurements.
  • Emphasis on reproducible workflows, open datasets, and modular software to support collaboration across projects and partners.

Teaching Multiscale, Physics-Based, and Data-Driven Learning

My teaching connects thermo-fluids, heat transfer, and mechanics with computation and data, using backward design, active learning, and inclusive practices. Students move through a cycle: concept → model → code → experiment → communication.

Teaching Philosophy

  • Backward design & mastery: clear outcomes in conceptual, mathematical, computational, and experimental skills, supported by frequent, low-stakes assessment.
  • Physics-informed use of data: students start from governing equations and models, then build ML surrogates that respect physical constraints.
  • Inclusive classrooms: structured group work, multiple modalities, and transparent rubrics.
  • Reproducible practice: clean code, documentation, and version control in all projects.
